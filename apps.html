Essential Apps

Install these before you land. Korea has its own app ecosystem — some Western defaults don't work as well here.

Navigation

Naver Maps
iOS · Android · Free
The go-to navigation app in Korea. Google Maps is limited here — Naver Maps has real-time transit, walking directions, and accurate local business info. Switch to English in settings.
Must install English supported
Subway Korea
iOS · Android · Free
Offline Seoul subway map with route planning in English. Works without data. Essential for navigating between conference venue and sightseeing spots.
Works offline English

Translation

Papago
iOS · Android · Free · By Naver
The best Korean↔English translator. Significantly more accurate than Google Translate for Korean. Has a camera mode — point at menus or signs to translate instantly. Download offline packs before arriving.
Must install Camera translate Offline mode

Getting Around

Kakao T
iOS · Android · Free
Korea's dominant taxi app. Works like Uber — tap, see fare estimate, get picked up. English interface available. Most reliable way to get a taxi, especially late at night. You can pay by card in-app.
Taxis English supported Card payment
T-money (or just use your card)
Physical card available at convenience stores · ~2,500 KRW
Prepaid transit card for subway and bus. Alternatively, most modern subway gates accept contactless credit/debit cards directly — tap your Visa/Mastercard and it works. No app needed if you go the card route.
Subway Bus

Food & Delivery

Baemin (배달의민족)
iOS · Android · Free
Korea's largest food delivery app. 30–60 minute delivery. Useful if you're staying in and don't want to go out. Interface is Korean but navigation is manageable — use Papago to help read menus.
Delivery Korean UI
Coupang Eats
iOS · Android · Free
Alternative to Baemin, often slightly faster. Same concept — delivery within the hour. Good selection around the Gangnam/COEX area.
Delivery Fast

Communication

KakaoTalk
iOS · Android · Free
The messaging app everyone in Korea uses — researchers, venue staff, locals. You'll likely be added to ICML group chats here. Install it before the conference starts.
Must install Everyone uses this

Connectivity tip

Get a Korean eSIM or SIM card at Incheon Airport (arrivals hall, multiple carriers). A 10-day data SIM costs roughly 15,000–25,000 KRW (~$11–18). Recommended carriers: KT, SKT, or LG U+. Alternatively, order an eSIM in advance from your carrier before flying.
