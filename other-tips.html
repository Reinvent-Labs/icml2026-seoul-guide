Practical Tips

Transit, prices, connectivity, and things to know before you go.

Getting to COEX

  • From Incheon Airport: Take AREX express train (~43 min) to Seoul Station, then subway to Samseong. Total ~1.5 hrs, ~10,000 KRW.
  • Subway: Samseong Station (Line 2, Exit 5 or 6) or Bongeunsa Station (Line 9, Exit 7)
  • By taxi: Use Kakao T app. From Gangnam ~10,000 KRW, from central Seoul ~15,000–20,000 KRW.

Money & Payments

  • Currency: Korean Won (KRW). 1 USD ≈ 1,350 KRW.
  • Cards: Visa/Mastercard accepted almost everywhere. Contactless works on subway gates too.
  • ATMs: GS25 and 7-Eleven convenience store ATMs reliably accept foreign cards. Airport ATMs also work.
  • Cash: Rarely needed — most places, even street food stalls, accept card. Carry ~20,000 KRW as backup.
  • Tipping: Not customary. Do not tip.

Food Prices

  • Local restaurant lunch: 8,000–15,000 KRW (~$6–11)
  • Americano at Korean chain (Hollys, Ediya, Mega): 1,500–2,500 KRW (~$1–2)
  • Starbucks: 4,500–7,000 KRW (~$3–5)
  • Convenience store meal: 3,000–6,000 KRW (~$2–4)
  • Anything above 30,000 KRW/person is considered expensive

Weather in July

  • Temperature: 27–35°C (80–95°F), very humid.
  • Rain: Monsoon season (장마, Jangma) — expect occasional heavy rain. Carry a small umbrella or get one at Daiso (1,000–2,000 KRW).
  • Indoor tip: Air conditioning is aggressive indoors — bring a light layer for conference halls.

Connectivity

  • SIM: Buy at Incheon Airport arrivals (KT, SKT, LG U+). 10-day data SIM ~15,000–25,000 KRW.
  • eSIM: Order before flying from your carrier or apps like Airalo.
  • Subway wifi: Free public wifi throughout the Seoul subway network.
  • Cafe wifi: Almost universal — password usually on the receipt or counter.

Power & Adapters

  • Voltage: 220V / 60Hz
  • Plug type: Type C and F (European-style, two round pins)
  • If you're from North America: You need an adapter. Buy at Daiso or the airport for ~2,000–5,000 KRW.

Shopping

  • Daiso: Korea's dollar store — adapters, umbrellas, stationary, everything cheap.
  • Olive Young: Korean skincare and pharmacy essentials. OTC medications available here too.
  • Convenience stores (CU, GS25, 7-Eleven): Open 24/7. Snacks, drinks, hot food, printing, ATM.

Restaurant tips

  • Many restaurants use kiosks for ordering — card or mobile pay only.
  • Others have a call button on the table to summon staff.
  • Water and banchan (side dishes) are usually free and self-serve.
  • Use Naver Map for local reviews, or search Instagram with #강남맛집 (Gangnam restaurants).
