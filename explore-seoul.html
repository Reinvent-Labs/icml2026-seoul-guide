Explore Seoul

Popular areas to explore around the city — grouped by what you're looking for.

Culture & History

Royal Palaces Area
Gyeongbokgung Station · Line 3
Five palaces: Gyeongbokgung, Changdeokgung, Changgyeonggung, Deoksugung, and Jongmyo Shrine. Rent a hanbok nearby — wearing one gets you free entry to all palaces.
History Free with hanbok
Bukchon Hanok Village
Anguk Station · Line 3
Traditional hanok houses on the hillside between two palaces. Best on foot — narrow alleys opening onto tiled rooftop views. Go early morning to avoid crowds.
Walking Photogenic

Trendy & Modern

Seongsu
Seongsu Station · Line 2 · Exit 3
Seoul's creative district. Old factories converted into cafes, concept stores, and pop-ups. Constantly changing — good for a wander without a plan.
Cafes Design Pop-ups
Hongdae
Hongik Univ. Station · Line 2
University neighborhood. Street food, live performances, bars, and energetic nightlife. Best in the evening. Very young and creative crowd.
Nightlife Street food
Myeongdong & Euljiro
Myeongdong Station · Line 4
Myeongdong for shopping and street food. Nearby Euljiro ("Hipjiro") has trendy cafes tucked between old printing shops — a great contrast.
Shopping Street food

Relaxed & Local

Han River Parks
Various stations along the Han River
Locals picnic by the river, especially on evenings and weekends. Order delivery food to the river (it works). Yeouido and Banpo are the most popular spots.
Outdoors Local vibe
Itaewon & Haebangchon
Itaewon Station · Line 6
Itaewon is Seoul's most international neighborhood — diverse food, English-speaking staff everywhere. Haebangchon (HBC) next door is quieter with hill-top cafes and city views.
International Diverse food
Yeouido
Yeouido Station · Line 5 or Line 9
The Hyundai Seoul department store (one of the most impressive in Asia), IFC Mall, and Yeouido Park along the Han River. Good mix of high-end shopping and outdoor space.
Shopping Park

Views

N Seoul Tower (Namsan)
Cable car from Myeongdong or hike up from Itaewon
Panoramic 360° views over the city. Go at dusk for best light. The hike up takes ~30 minutes if you skip the cable car.
Views Iconic
Lotte World Tower Observatory
Jamsil Station · Line 2 / 8
123-floor skyscraper with a glass floor section. Seoul's tallest building. Book tickets in advance — can sell out on weekends.
Views Book ahead
