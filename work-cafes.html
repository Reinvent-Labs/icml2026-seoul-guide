Work Cafes Near COEX

Good spots to write, prep a talk, or just think — within walking distance or a short subway ride from the conference venue.

What to look for

Korean cafe culture is researcher-friendly: most places have strong wifi, plentiful outlets, and won't rush you out. Look for 스터디카페 (study cafes) if you need silence — these are library-style spaces where you rent a desk by the hour (~2,000–3,000 KRW/hr).

Tips for working in Korean cafes

  • Most cafes expect at least one purchase per person — order a coffee if you're staying a while.
  • Peak crowding: 12pm–2pm on weekdays. Arrive before or after lunch.
  • Wifi password is usually on the receipt or printed near the counter — ask "와이파이 비밀번호가 뭐예요?" (wifi password?)
  • Naver Maps shows real-time busyness — check before walking over.
  • Many cafes close relatively early (9–10pm) — plan accordingly for evening sessions.
